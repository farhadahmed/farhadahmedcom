Experience

I've been a software engineer since 2016. My path began when I was a claims processor making less than $25K a year; not enough a person to live on, and I was about to get married.

I saved enough to pay my bills and take some courses, I quit my job, and dedicated two years to doing nothing but studying programming. I threw out my Xbox, told my friends they won't see me for a year, and when my brother-in-law was married, I didn't attend his wedding. Because for those two years, from the time I woke up to time I slept, I was coding. My meals were all eaten with one hand on my fork and the other on my keyboard. I'd fall asleep trying to solve algorithms with my eyes closed. I developed code until I'd see it in my dreams.

I have maintained this motivation throughout my career. At Apex Learning I shortened their deployment time for API credentials from one week to one hour. At Comcast I built the web app that Roku uses to translate their .csv files an XML file that Comcast uses to ingest and publish media to their platform. At Skillspire I created the curriculum they now use for all Full-Stack JavaScript courses. At Sterling I built and maintained the entire integration experience for our client's developers.
